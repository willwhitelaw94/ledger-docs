## What Is March Madness?

A **week-long hackathon** where anyone at Trilogy Care can build something useful with AI. Not a coding competition—a **value creation** competition.

Open to **everyone**: Business, Product, and Development.

You don't need to be a developer. You need an idea and the ability to describe what you want.

---

## The Rules

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Team Size** | 1-3 people |
| **Submission Deadline** | 2 weeks before the hackathon |
| **Build Week** | One full week to create your solution |
| **Demo Day** | Present what you built |

### Why Teams of 1-3?

Think **pair prompting**—the AI-era equivalent of pair programming. One person describes, another reviews, a third tests. Or go solo if that's your style.

## Judging Criteria

Projects will be assessed across four dimensions:

| Criteria | Weight | What We're Looking For |
|----------|--------|------------------------|
| **Business Value** | 40% | Does this solve a real problem? How much time/money does it save? |
| **Innovation** | 25% | Is this a creative approach? Did you think differently? |
| **Execution** | 20% | Does it work? Is it polished? Could someone use it today? |
| **Wow Factor** | 15% | Did it make us say "holy shit"? |

### Business Value Examples

- Automates something that takes hours → now takes minutes
- Surfaces information that was previously hidden
- Reduces errors in a manual process
- Improves customer/coordinator experience
- Saves the company money

## What Can You Build?

### Track: Business & Product

For non-developers—use Claude Cowork, Claude Desktop, or any AI tool to build:

- Automated reports and dashboards
- Data aggregation from multiple systems
- Document generation (board packs, summaries, templates)
- Process automation tools
- Chrome extensions and browser tools
- Spreadsheet wizardry with AI

**You don't need to touch the portal codebase.** Build standalone tools that solve real problems.

### Track: Development

For developers—build within the **TC Portal ecosystem or apps**:

- New portal features or improvements
- Claude Code skills for common workflows
- MCP integrations we don't have yet
- Internal dev tools and debugging aids
- API integrations and automations
- Performance or monitoring tools

**Your project must integrate with or extend our existing systems.**

### Both Tracks

Solve something that frustrates you or your team:

- A report that takes forever to compile
- Information scattered across systems
- Manual processes that should be automated
- Data that's hard to find or understand
- Better visibility into key metrics

## Real Example: Care Vicinity Chrome Extension

**Builder:** Kiel

**The Problem:** Leadership wanted to see the most important metric—how many jobs were filled this week—without logging into multiple systems.

**The Solution:** A Chrome extension that:
- Installs on any computer
- Shows jobs filled this week at a glance
- Updates automatically
- Lives in the browser toolbar

**Time to Build:** One session with Claude Code

**Business Value:** Instant visibility for leadership without context-switching

This is the kind of thing that wins. Not complex. Not over-engineered. Just **useful**.

## How to Enter

### Step 1: Submit Your Idea (2 Weeks Before)

Tell us:
- **What you're building** (one sentence)
- **What problem it solves** (one paragraph)
- **Who benefits** (coordinators? leadership? providers?)
- **Your team** (1-3 people)

### Step 2: Build It (Hackathon Week)

You have the full week. Use Claude Code, Cursor, Cowork—whatever tools you want.

### Step 3: Demo Day

Present your creation:
- **5 minutes** to show what you built
- **2 minutes** for questions
- Live demo preferred (recordings accepted if necessary)

## Tips for Success

### Start Small

The Chrome extension example above is simple. It does one thing well. Don't try to build a platform—build a solution.

### Solve Your Own Problem

The best projects come from real frustration. What annoys you? What takes too long? What information do you wish you had?

### Use What You Know

You don't need to learn new technology. Use the tools you already have access to:
- [Claude Code](/ways-of-working/claude-code) for building
- [Claude Cowork](/ways-of-working/overview/04-cowork-connectors) for non-technical approaches
- [MCP Integrations](/ways-of-working/mcps/01-overview) for connecting systems

### Focus on the Demo

A working demo beats a perfect plan. Get something functional, then polish.

## FAQ

**Do I need to be a developer?**
No. Many of the best projects will come from people closest to the problems—CS, operations, finance, coordination.

**Can I use existing code or tools?**
Yes. Build on what exists. The goal is value, not reinvention.

**What if my idea doesn't work?**
That's fine. Show us what you tried and what you learned. Failure with insight beats nothing attempted.

**Can I work on this during work hours?**
Yes—that's the point of the hackathon week.

**What happens to the projects after?**
Good projects get adopted. Great projects get built into our tools.
