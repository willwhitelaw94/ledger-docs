As described in the Scrum Guide:

> "The purpose of the Daily Scrum is to inspect progress toward the Sprint Goal and adapt the Sprint Backlog as necessary, adjusting the upcoming planned work."

---

## The Problem with Stale Standups

Humans get bored doing the same thing over and over. As boredom sinks in, so does engagement—and soon daily standups start to feel like:

- A waste of time
- Not getting any insights/value on progress towards the goal
- Status meetings where every team member just updates what they're working on

---

## Three Formats to Keep Things Fresh

We rotate between three different formats. Each has its own strengths.

### 1. Standard Format

The most common style. Go by participant or by tickets on the board (right to left).

Each participant states:

- What they did yesterday
- What they're planning to do today
- Any blockers

**Best for:** Teams new to standups, or when individual context is important.

---

### 2. Walk the Board

Start from the right side of the board and move left, discussing each story.

**Focus areas:**

- Less time on "In Progress" tickets (unless blocked)
- More emphasis on **cycle time**—what can we do to move tickets to Done?
- Call out the **age of each ticket** and time in current swimlane
- Review overall **WIP on the board**

Each participant states:

- Any collaborative effort required to move cards to Done
- Blockers preventing progress

**Best for:** Mature teams focused on flow and delivery metrics.

---

### 3. Text-Based Standup

::callout{color="amber" icon="i-lucide-message-square-text"}
**Warning**: Overuse of this style will cause issues long-term. Usually once or twice a week is an OK cadence.
::

Unlike real-time standups, text-based versions allow team members to contribute updates at their convenience. Especially beneficial for teams spread across time zones.

**Structure** (same classic questions):

- What did you accomplish yesterday?
- What will you be working on today?
- Are there any blockers or impediments?

**Automation**: Many teams automate text-based standups with bots that provide:

- Predefined structure
- Reminders to complete by a certain time
- Happiness score collection

Example tool: [Sup Bot](https://www.supbot.com/)

---

## Text-Based Standup Benefits

| Benefit                  | Description                                                                           |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Flexibility**          | Accommodates diverse schedules and time zones                                         |
| **Documentation**        | Written record of daily progress for tracking and accountability                      |
| **Reduced Distractions** | Eliminates real-time meetings, minimising workflow interruptions                      |
| **Increased Focus**      | Writing updates adds thought and planning to the daily process                        |
| **Inclusivity**          | Allows more even contribution, especially from those less likely to speak up verbally |

---

## Tips for Effective Standups

### Do

- Keep it **under 15 minutes**
- Stand up (if in person)—it keeps things short
- Focus on the **Sprint Goal**, not just individual tasks
- Surface blockers immediately
- Rotate formats to keep engagement high

### Don't

- Turn it into a problem-solving session (take those offline)
- Let one person dominate
- Skip standups when things are "going well"
- Make it a status report to management
