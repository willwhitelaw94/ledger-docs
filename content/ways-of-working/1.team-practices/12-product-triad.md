---
title: "Product Triad Meeting"
description: Weekly cross-domain sync between Design, Tech, and Business
---


Bring Designer, Tech Lead, and Business together to triage upcoming work and move items cleanly from Idea → PRD → Production with no surprises.

## Why

By discussing work from tech, design, and business perspectives up front, we reduce risk of delivery going sideways later.

**Goal:** Design the right thing. Build the thing right.

## How

- Short, regular syncs (30-45 min weekly)
- Co-shape problems and options before locking in solutions
- Carry shared context into delivery

**Outcome:** Less pressure on handovers. Less creep. Less rework.

---

## Cadence

Weekly, 30-40 minutes

## Attendees

| Role | Focus |
|------|-------|
| Designer | Desirability - Users, flows, edge cases, constraints |
| Tech Lead | Feasibility - Risks, dependencies, architecture, sizing |
| Business Analyst | Viability - Problem statement, value, outcomes, appetite |

---

## Agenda

### 1. Kick-off & Priorities (2 mins)

Confirm the top 2-4 items in the pipeline. What's priority to move into delivery next?

*Move the right items forward; park the rest.*

### 2. Pipeline Snapshot (5 mins)

Review each backlog item stage:

| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Ideas** | Raw thought. No homework done yet. |
| **Discovery** | Poking it to see if it's worth doing. Light research, UX sketches, tech feasibility, business value checks. |
| **Draft PRD** | First-pass write-up of what we plan to build and why. Scope, users, flows, acceptance criteria, risks, dependencies. |

Note blockers, dependencies, and deadlines.

### 3. Item Deep-Dives (20-25 mins)

For each priority item, time-box to 6-7 mins:

**Each role contributes:**
- **BA:** Problem statement, business value, key outcomes, appetite for effort/cost
- **Designer:** Users, key flows, edge cases, must-have constraints
- **Tech Lead:** Feasibility, risks, dependencies, technical approach, rough sizing

**Decide one outcome:**

| Decision | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **Advance** | "Idea is good, let's draft a PRD" or "PRD is complete - into delivery backlog" |
| **Explore** | "Needs a tech spike / interview / prototype / SME research" |
| **Park** | "Not a priority or unclear—revisit later" |

Capture actions with owners and due dates.

### 4. Devil's Advocate Sweep (3-5 mins)

Ask:
- What's the riskiest assumption?
- What would break in production?
- What's the simplest viable version?

If the answer is hand-wavy, log an action to validate.

### 5. Wrap & Next Steps (3 mins)

- Recap decisions and actions
- Confirm updates to backlog
- Set expectations for next week

---

## Working Agreements

- **One page or it didn't happen:** Keep discovery notes and draft PRDs concise
- **Time-boxed debates:** If it's taking longer than 3 minutes, spin off an action
- **Evidence beats opinion:** Link to data, interviews, or spikes
- **Smallest viable first:** Prefer MVP path; fancy can come later
- **Clear ownership:** Every action has a single owner and a date

---

## Inputs to Bring

| Role | Bring |
|------|-------|
| BA | Problem statements, success metrics, stakeholder notes |
| Designer | User flows/wireframes, known UX constraints |
| Tech Lead | Architecture notes, dependency map, spike outcomes |

## Outputs Each Week

- Updated pipeline status (Idea / Discovery / Draft PRD)
- Short action list with owners and dates
- Named items moving into Draft PRD, ready for refinement

---

## Lightweight Templates

**Problem Statement:**
> For [user], the problem is [x], which causes [y]. Success = [metric].

**Scope Boundaries:**
> Included: [a, b]. Excluded: [c].

**MVP Cut:**
> Earliest shippable version is [feature set], risks are [r1, r2], validation via [method].

**Tech Notes:**
> Dependencies: [systems/teams]. Risks: [perf, data, integration]. Spike: [1-2 days].

---

## Common Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Too many items | Cap deep-dives to 4; the rest roll over |
| Vague value | Ask for a metric or user outcome; otherwise park it |
| Design perfectionism | Start with flows/wires; polish later |
| Tech rabbit holes | Create a spike, not a debate |
| Wandering scope | Use "Included/Excluded" every time |

---

## Success Signals

- Clear, small PRDs ready for refinement each week
- Fewer surprises in sprint planning
- Faster decisions; fewer back-and-forths
- Stakeholders know what's coming and why

**Short, sharp, and useful. If it stops being any of those, we tweak.**
