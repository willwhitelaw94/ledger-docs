## A Generational Technology Shift

Every decade or so, a technology comes along that fundamentally reshapes how software gets built—and who survives.

| Era                  | The Shift            | Who Won                          |
| -------------------- | -------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| **1990s - Internet** | Desktop → Web        | Companies that went online       |
| **2010s - Mobile**   | Web → Apps           | Companies that went mobile-first |
| **2015s - Cloud**    | On-prem → Cloud      | Companies that embraced SaaS     |
| **2020s - AI**       | Manual → AI-assisted | Companies adapting now           |

**AI is that kind of shift.** This isn't "learn a new framework." This is "restructure how your team builds software."

:youtube-video{#g_Bvo0tsD9s title="Agentic AI is Inevitable"}

As developers and product designers who've been making products for many years in a traditional way, we need to be comfortable with the reality:

**This is a paradigm shift.**

Whether you come on board or not, it will be happening around you. The cat is out of the bag.

**You are in the best position to be the ones building beautiful houses.** The ones who understand foundations, architecture, and craft.

## AI Is Coming TO Your Job, Not FOR It

AI is coming *to* your job, not *for* it.

What AI can do:

- Write boilerplate in seconds
- Search codebases instantly
- Generate tests from specifications
- Explain unfamiliar code

What it can't do:

- Understand your business
- Make architectural decisions with context
- Know when requirements don't make sense
- Take responsibility for the system

**You're still the engineer.** You just have a much more powerful set of tools.

## The House Analogy: Engineer-Guided AI vs Vibe Coding

Think about building a multi-story house.

**Without proper engineering:**

- No structural engineer → the second story might collapse
- No architect → the toilet ends up on the kitchen bench
- Just vibes → it might hold up for a bit, but it won't work long-term

**With proper engineering:**

- **Structural engineer** ensures when you add the second story, the thing doesn't fall over
- **Architect** ensures the kitchen and bathroom have good flow
- **Builder** executes the plan efficiently

This is exactly how we think about AI-assisted development:

| Role                    | House Building          | Software Building                                |
| ----------------------- | ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| **Structural Engineer** | Ensures stability       | Engineers architecting solutions for scalability |
| **Architect**           | Designs flow and layout | Designers ensuring UX flows make sense           |
| **Builder**             | Executes the plan       | AI implementing the specified features           |

**The engineers and designers at Trilogy are the structural engineers and architects.** You're making sure that when AI builds the "second story," the whole thing doesn't fall over.

AI can prefab rooms quickly. But someone needs to ensure:

- The foundation can support the weight
- The plumbing connects properly
- The electrical is up to code
- The rooms flow logically

**Vibe coding** = AI builds a house with no plans. It might look okay at first, but good luck adding a second story.

**Engineer-guided AI** = AI builds efficiently from solid plans. The structure scales. The code maintains.

## Being the Complete Engineer

The engineers who thrive aren't just coders. They're problem solvers who operate across three dimensions:

| Dimension      | Focus                                                            |
| -------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Technical**  | System design, code quality, security, scalability               |
| **Product**    | What problem are we solving? Is this the right solution?         |
| **Commercial** | How does this impact revenue/costs? What's the opportunity cost? |

**The 8-8-8 Engineer**: Be 8/10 across all three, not 10/10 technical and 6/10 everything else.

AI raises the floor on technical execution. It doesn't help you understand whether you're building something worth building.

## The Marginal Cost Thesis

Every technology revolution has been about **dropping marginal costs**:

| Revolution     | What Got Cheaper         | Result                           |
| -------------- | ------------------------ | -------------------------------- |
| Printing Press | Copying text             | Mass literacy, reformation       |
| Internet       | Distributing information | Every company is a media company |
| Cloud          | Running servers          | Every startup can scale globally |
| **AI Coding**  | **Writing software**     | **Build will beat buy**          |

When something becomes nearly free, the entire economic structure built around its scarcity **collapses and rebuilds**.

**The marginal cost of software development is approaching zero. This changes everything.**

## Vibe Coding: The Viral Moment

The term "vibe coding" captured a shift that's been building for years. Developers describe *what* they want in natural language, not *how* to build it.

### Who's Doing It

- **Linus Torvalds** (Linux, Git creator): "Is this much better than I could do by hand? Sure is."
- **Google CEO** and **Klarna CEO**: Publicly embracing vibe coding
- **Claude Code**: Hit $1B annual run rate six months after launch
- **OpenAI, Anthropic, Intuit, SAP**: All actively supporting this trend

> **The insight:** Technical barriers to coding are no longer the drivers of software moats. Determining *what to build* and *how it should function within a broader system* is now more important than the technical act of building it.

## The Death of the Dashboard

### From Service Delivery to Intelligence Delivery

> "In 2026, the conversation shifts from 'SaaS with AI features' to 'AI-native SaaS.' New platforms won't bolt AI on top—they'll be architected around foundation models, inference pipelines, and continuous context loops from day zero."

### Your UI Is No Longer Your Moat

A provocative take from industry analysts:

> "Today, your UI is part of your moat. In 2026, your UI is only a reference implementation. If your data structures, APIs, and semantics are clean, customers will generate their own interfaces on top of you—using their design systems and their internal models."

**What this means:**

- Your product becomes one of many behind a universal agent
- Your navigation bar loses power
- Your dashboards lose influence
- **Your substrate becomes the only thing that matters**

### Two or Three Durable Surfaces

> "SaaS apps shrink to two or three durable surfaces. Everything else is generated, shared, discarded, and recreated. The winner is not the prettiest UI. The winner is the system agents can choreograph most easily."

## 2026: The Year of AI Agents

AI is evolving from **passive tools** (chat UI waiting for prompts) to **active agentic systems** (software that plans, executes, and iterates autonomously).

### The UX Inversion

| Traditional UX                        | AI-Native UX                     |
| ------------------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Conversational UI (ask AI a question) | Delegative UI (assign AI a goal) |
| Design for human clicks               | Design for agent orchestration   |
| Pretty dashboards                     | Clean APIs and semantics         |
| Feature-rich interfaces               | Minimal durable surfaces         |
| Users navigate workflows              | Agents choreograph systems       |

### The New Design Challenge

> "The next great UX challenge is not designing the prompt interface, but designing the **Audit Interface**—to summarize an agent's 50-step chain of thought into a single, glanceable confidence check for the human manager."

## What This Means for TC Portal

Taylor's framework maps directly to our situation:

- **"Jobs to be done"**: What job does TC Portal get hired to do? It's not "display fields in a browser"—it's enable better care outcomes.
- **Incumbent risk**: "Incumbents are seeing churn at the edges. AI-native products sit on top of existing systems, pull context across them, and create value the platform doesn't capture."
- **The question**: Will we be the AI-native layer, or will someone else build it on top of us?

### The Opportunity

If marginal cost of software → zero, then:

1. **Speed of experimentation** becomes the differentiator
2. **Domain expertise** (aged care, Support at Home) becomes the moat
3. **Data substrate quality** matters more than UI polish
4. **Being orchestrable** (clean APIs, MCP) becomes essential

### The Risk

If we remain a traditional dashboard-driven SaaS:

- AI-native competitors could sit on top of our data
- Capture the value while we provide the substrate
- Reduce TC Portal to a "dumb pipe" for care management data
